Compliance Duties

Non-negotiable checkpoints for every transaction. Miss one and the sale stops.

  • Verify 21+ before engagement: Ask for ID prior to discussing product or ringing a sale. 28-B §504(4)(A)
  • Acceptable ID only: Government-issued photo ID or options approved by rule; no expired documents. 28-B §504(4)(B)
  • Total weight ≤ 2.5 oz: Combine flower, concentrates, infused products, and accessories that contain cannabis. 28-B §504(2)(B)
  • Concentrate ≤ 10 g: Cartridges, dabs, oils, etc. share this cap, even if total weight is low. 28-B §1501(1)(B)
  • Decline intoxicated customers: Stop the sale, document, notify leadership. 28-B §504(2)(D)
  • Prohibited items: No mature plants, and no cannabis products combined with tobacco or alcohol. 28-B §504(2)(A)
  • Human-operated only: No vending or unattended dispensing machines. 28-B §504(2)(C)(1)

Customer Interaction Scripts

Use these scripts for common scenarios. Keep responses short, cite the rule, and escalate as needed.

  • ID check: “Hi! Before we get started, I’ll need to see a valid government-issued photo ID.” 28-B §504(4)
  • Purchase limit explanation: “Maine law sets a maximum of 2.5 ounces total and 10 grams of concentrate per transaction. I’ll help you stay compliant.” 28-B §1501(1)(B)
  • Declining a sale: “I’m sorry, I can’t complete this sale. If you have questions, my manager can explain the rules.” 28-B §504(2)(D)
  • Product questions: “I can share product details, but I can’t make medical claims. For medical advice, please consult a healthcare professional.”
  • Recalls or compliance alerts: “This product is currently under review. I’ll help you find a compliant alternative.”

Incident Handling & Escalation

Follow these steps for common incidents. Document and escalate per SOP.

  • Failed ID scan: Log the incident, verify manually, and escalate to manager if unresolved. 28-B §504(4)
  • Suspected diversion or theft: Document, notify manager, and report to OCP as required. 28-B §303
  • Inventory discrepancy: Log, attempt reconciliation, escalate unresolved issues to manager and OCP. 28-B §105
  • Customer dispute: Remain calm, cite the rule, and offer to escalate to management.

Product Knowledge

Stay current on product types, labeling, and compliance. Reference METRC and OCP guidance for details.

  • Flower: Whole or ground cannabis plant material. 28-B §701
  • Concentrate: Extracted cannabinoids (oils, dabs, cartridges). 28-B §701
  • Edible: Infused food or beverage. 28-B §703(1)(F)
  • Infused product: Non-edible products containing cannabis (topicals, tinctures). 28-B §701
  • Labeling: License number, batch ID, net weight, potency, universal symbol, warnings. 28-B §701

Accessibility & Print Features

This guide is designed to work for everyone, whether you use a mouse, keyboard, screen reader, or need to print reference materials for the counter.

Using This Page with Assistive Technology

  • Screen readers: Navigate by heading (H key) to jump between sections. All images have descriptive alt text.
  • Keyboard navigation: Press Tab to move through links and buttons. Press Enter to activate. Use Shift+Tab to go back.
  • Skip navigation: Press Tab when the page loads to access a "Skip to content" link that jumps past the header.
  • High contrast: All text meets WCAG AA standards for readability against backgrounds.
  • Focus indicators: A visible outline appears around interactive elements when you tab to them.

Printing This Guide

  • Print-optimized layout: Use your browser's print function (Ctrl+P or Cmd+P) to generate a clean, readable copy.
  • Navigation hidden: The header and navigation menu automatically hide when printing to save space.
  • Clear sections: Each section starts on a clear break with proper spacing for easy reference.
  • Save as PDF: Use "Save as PDF" in your print dialog to create a digital copy for tablets or phones.

Consumption Methods

  • • Inhalation: onset in minutes, shorter duration.
  • • Edibles: 30–120 minute onset, longer lasting—stress patience.
  • • Topicals: localized relief, non-intoxicating when used properly.

Dosage Guidelines

  • • Start low (2.5–5 mg THC) for new consumers.
  • • Track total THC per serving and per package for each recommendation.
