• Legal Authority: Maine's medical marijuana program operates under Title 22 MRSA Chapter 558-C, distinct from the adult-use program (Title 28-B). Do not confuse statutes.
  • Possession Limits: Medical patients may possess up to 8 pounds of prepared marijuana (NOT 2.5 oz like adult-use), plus 6 mature and 12 immature plants.
  • Caregiver Operations: Each registered caregiver may assist a maximum of 5 qualifying patients simultaneously per Title 22 §2423-A(2)(C).
  • Visiting Patients: Out-of-state patients with valid registry cards limited to 2.5 oz per 15-day period per Title 22 §2423-A(2)(B-1).
  • Record Retention: All transaction records, patient verification documentation, and compliance logs must be maintained for minimum 5 years per Title 22 §2430-G.
  • Packaging Standards: Child-resistant, tamper-evident, opaque packaging required (Title 22 §2429-A). Multi-serving liquids require measurement components.
  • Advertising Restrictions: No signs within 1,000 ft of schools (or 500-1,000 ft per municipal variance), no targeting under-21, no false health claims (Title 22 §2429-B).

Required Signage and Staff References

Medical marijuana dispensaries and registered caregivers operating under Maine's Medical Use of Cannabis Act (Title 22 MRSA Chapter 558-C) have specific posting, notice, and compliance requirements distinct from the adult-use program. All materials below open dedicated print pages optimized for letter-size paper.

  • Legal Framework: Title 22 MRSA Chapter 558-C (Medical Use of Cannabis Act)
  • Rules: Maine OCP Chapter 4 (Medical Marijuana Program)
  • Citations: All references cite Title 22 statutes, NOT adult-use Title 28-B
  • Print Format: Unified layout, accessible markup, optimized for grayscale printing

Qualifying Conditions Information

As of 2022, Maine physicians have full discretion to certify any debilitating medical condition they believe could benefit from medical marijuana. Commonly certified conditions include chronic pain, PTSD, cancer, epilepsy, glaucoma, HIV/AIDS, ALS, Alzheimer's, and multiple sclerosis. Educational reference for patient inquiries.

Product Packaging Requirements

All medical marijuana products must be in child-resistant, tamper-evident, and opaque packaging. Multi-serving liquid products require measurement components with child-resistant caps. Checklist includes CPSC standards compliance, prohibited imagery (no humans, animals, fruits), and verification that labeling cannot appeal to individuals under 21.
