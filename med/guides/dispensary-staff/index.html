Patient-focused compliance, from verifying medical cards to dispensing medicine.

Core Duties: Patient Care & Compliance

As dispensary staff or a registered caregiver, your primary role is to safely and legally provide medical cannabis to qualified patients. This guide covers the essential procedures.

Patient Verification

Verifying Patient Status

Before dispensing, you must verify the patient's status by inspecting their valid registry identification card and a valid photographic identification. Title 22, §2425

Purchase & Possession Limits

Calculating Purchase Limits

A qualifying patient may not possess more than 2.5 ounces of prepared marijuana. All dispensing must be accurately recorded and tracked against this limit. Title 22, §2423-A

Patient & Caregiver Cultivation

Registered patients or their designated primary caregivers are permitted to cultivate up to six mature marijuana plants for the patient's use. Ensure any advice given to patients aligns with these legal limits.

Key Medical Program Terms

The medical program uses specific terminology. Understanding these terms is critical for compliance.

  • Caregiver: A person, 21 years or older, registered with OCP to provide care and dispense cannabis for qualified patients. Caregivers may hire employees and serve multiple patients.
  • Patient: A person with a valid medical certification for a qualifying condition, allowing them to possess up to 2.5 ounces of cannabis and cultivate up to six mature plants.
  • OCP: Office of Cannabis Policy, the regulatory body for the Maine Medical Use of Marijuana Program.
  • Qualifying Condition: A medical condition specified in state law that allows a patient to use medical cannabis.
