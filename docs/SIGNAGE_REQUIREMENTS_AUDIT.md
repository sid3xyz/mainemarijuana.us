# Maine Cannabis Signage Requirements Audit
**Date:** October 13, 2025  
**Audited by:** Statute verification against current Maine law  
**Data extracted:** January 7, 2025

## Executive Summary

**CRITICAL FINDING:** Maine statute mandates **ONLY ONE facility posting** for both adult-use and medical cannabis programs:

- **Adult-Use (Title 28-B §509):** License must be conspicuously displayed within licensed premises
- **Medical (Title 22 §2428(6)(K)):** Registration certificate must be displayed in a publicly visible location in the dispensary

**ALL OTHER SIGNAGE** previously labeled as "Required" on this site is **RECOMMENDED best practice** but NOT statutorily mandated.

## Detailed Statute Analysis

### Adult-Use Program (Title 28-B)

#### Title 28-B §509: License to be conspicuously displayed
**TEXT:** "A licensee shall ensure that the licensee's license, or a copy of that license, is at all times conspicuously displayed within its licensed premises."

**RESULT:** ✅ **ONLY STATUTORY FACILITY SIGNAGE REQUIREMENT**

#### Title 28-B §504: Operation of cannabis stores
- **§504(4):** Requires age verification **PRACTICE** (employee must verify government-issued ID)
- **DOES NOT require posted signage** - operational requirement only
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate

#### Title 28-B §508: Use of cannabis and cannabis products within licensed premises
- **§508(3):** Prohibits consumption on premises (operational rule)
- **DOES NOT require posted signage** - just the prohibition itself
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate

#### Title 28-B §701: Labeling and packaging
- **§701(1)(C):** Requires health and safety warning labels on **PRODUCT PACKAGING**
- **This is PRODUCT labeling, NOT facility signage**
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate (product labels only)

#### Title 28-B §702: Signs, advertising and marketing
- **§702(1):** RESTRICTIONS on advertising (prohibits misleading ads, ads near schools)
- **This restricts advertising, does NOT mandate facility signage**
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate (advertising restrictions only)

### Medical Program (Title 22)

#### Title 22 §2428(6)(K): Registered dispensaries
**TEXT:** "A dispensary shall display the dispensary's registration certificate issued under section 2425-A in a publicly visible location in the dispensary."

**RESULT:** ✅ **ONLY STATUTORY FACILITY SIGNAGE REQUIREMENT**

#### Title 22 §2423-A: Authorized conduct
- Defines qualifying patient possession limits, caregiver rights, cultivation allowances
- **DOES NOT require posted signage** - defines legal conduct only
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate

#### Title 22 §2426: Scope
- Defines limitations (cannot use in schools, operate vehicles under influence, etc.)
- **DOES NOT require posted signage** - scope limitations only
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate

#### Title 22 §2429-B: Signs, advertising and marketing
- RESTRICTIONS on dispensary advertising (similar to adult-use §702)
- **This restricts advertising, does NOT mandate facility signage**
- **RESULT:** ❌ No facility signage mandate (advertising restrictions only)

## Verification Methodology

1. **Direct statute fetch** from legislature.maine.gov (authoritative source)
2. **Search for signage language:** "shall post", "must display", "signage", "sign", "notice"
3. **Distinguish:**
   - Statutory signage mandates (found: license/certificate display only)
   - Product labeling requirements (§701 - on packages, not facilities)
   - Operational requirements (rules to follow, not signage)
   - Advertising restrictions (limits on ads, not signage requirements)
   - Best practices (recommended but not legally required)

## Why This Matters

### Legal Compliance
- **Mandatory vs Optional:** Establishments must know what's legally required vs helpful but voluntary
- **Risk mitigation:** Over-claiming requirements creates unnecessary burden; under-claiming creates legal risk
- **Enforcement:** OCP can cite for missing license display; cannot cite for missing "recommended" signage

### Best Practice Recommendation
**We STRONGLY RECOMMEND posting all signage** even though only license display is mandated:
- **Customer education:** Helps communicate legal limits and requirements
- **Staff training:** Visual reminders of operational rules
- **Good-faith compliance:** Demonstrates proactive compliance efforts
- **Liability protection:** Shows customers were informed of rules/risks
- **Inspection readiness:** Demonstrates operational knowledge
